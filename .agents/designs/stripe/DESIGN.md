# Design System: Stripe

## Overview
Stripe's design system is the global benchmark for fintech and SaaS aesthetics. It manages to feel simultaneously high-tech and high-luxury, balancing mathematical precision with fluid, organic motion.

## Design Philosophy
1. **The Gold Standard:** Every pixel is intentional. Alignment, spacing, and typography are tuned to a level of "perfect" clarity.
2. **Technical Luxury:** Uses premium typography (Soehne) and complex gradients to elevate "boring" financial infrastructure into a desirable experience.
3. **Fluidity & Motion:** Design is not static. It uses "Sash" gradients and smooth transitions to imply the speed of global capital.
4. **Confident Restraint:** High use of whitespace and a limited, purposeful color palette.

## Colors
- **Stripe Blurple (#635bff):** The iconic brand color. It is vibrant yet professional, designed to stand out against white and dark navy backgrounds.
- **The Navy Foundation:** Dark mode is not black; it's a deep, rich navy (`#0a2540`) that feels more "institutional" and trustworthy.
- **Vibrant Accents:** Uses cyan, pink, and yellow sparingly in gradients to represent different product lines.

## Typography
- **Geometric Refinement:** Prioritizes clean, Neo-Grotesk typefaces with generous tracking for readability.
- **Hierarchy:** Uses font weight (300 to 500) and scale to create a clear path through complex data without relying on bold colors.

## Components
- **The Stripe Card:** A white surface with a very subtle 1px border and a soft, multi-layered shadow.
- **Interactive Gradients:** Buttons often feature subtle linear gradients and a distinctive "press" state.
- **The "Sash":** Slanted background sections (usually at 12 degrees) used to create dynamic flow between marketing chapters.

## Visual Effects
- **Multi-layered Shadows:** Uses multiple shadow steps to create "real" depth on cards and modals.
- **Animated Gradients:** Backgrounds often feature slow-moving, multi-colored mesh gradients (the "Stripe Glow").
- **Precision Borders:** 1px borders are used with high contrast against the background to define structures.
